# Retail Sales Performance and Customer Insights Analysis

## Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end retail sales analysis using Microsoft Excel. The objective of the project is to transform raw retail transaction data into meaningful business insights through data cleaning, feature engineering, KPI analysis, PivotTables, and data visualization.

The analysis focuses on sales performance, customer behavior, product categories, sales channels, payment preferences, discounts, quarterly trends, and product returns.

The dataset contains approximately 30,000 retail order records with multiple attributes related to customers, products, transactions, delivery, marketing, and customer satisfaction.

## Project Objectives

The main objectives of this project are:

- Analyze overall retail sales performance
- Identify top-performing cities and product categories
- Understand revenue contribution across different sales channels
- Analyze customer segments and their contribution to revenue
- Identify customer payment preferences
- Analyze discount patterns across product categories
- Track quarterly sales performance
- Analyze product return behavior
- Compare product demand based on quantity sold
- Generate actionable business insights from the data

## Data Cleaning

The raw dataset was cleaned and prepared before performing the analysis.

The following data cleaning steps were performed:

- Identified and handled missing values
- Standardized categorical values
- Cleaned and formatted date columns
- Reviewed numerical columns for invalid or inconsistent values
- Handled missing categorical information appropriately
- Reviewed customer ratings and other business-related fields
- Ensured the dataset was suitable for PivotTable and chart analysis

## Feature Engineering

New calculated fields were created to improve the analysis.

### Total Sales

Total Sales was calculated using:

Quantity × Unit Price

This represents the sales value before applying discounts.

### Final Sales

Final Sales was calculated after applying the discount percentage to Total Sales.

This represents the actual revenue generated after discounts.

### Discount Amount

Discount Amount was calculated as the difference between Total Sales and Final Sales.

### Quarter

A Quarter field was created from the order date to analyze sales performance over time.

Examples:

2023-Q1  
2024-Q2  
2025-Q3

## Analysis and Visualizations

### Top 10 Cities by Final Sales

This analysis identifies the cities generating the highest final sales.

Bengaluru recorded the highest final sales among the top cities. Pune and Ahmedabad also showed strong sales performance.

The analysis helps identify important geographic markets where customer spending and revenue generation are relatively high.

### Final Sales by Product Category

This analysis compares revenue performance across different product categories.

Grocery generated the highest final sales, followed by other strong-performing categories such as Furniture and Books.

The results can help businesses prioritize inventory, marketing, and promotional strategies based on category performance.

### Revenue Contribution by Sales Channel

Revenue was analyzed across Website, Mobile App, Marketplace, and Offline sales channels.

Website and Mobile App were the dominant channels, each contributing approximately 38 percent of total revenue.

The analysis indicates that digital channels play an important role in overall business revenue.

### Revenue by Customer Segment

Customer segments were compared based on their revenue contribution.

Regular customers generated the highest revenue, while New customers also contributed significantly.

This analysis helps identify valuable customer groups and can support customer retention and targeting strategies.

### Preferred Payment Methods

Payment preferences were analyzed based on order volume.

Debit Card was the most frequently used payment method, while COD, Credit Card, UPI, Wallet, and Net Banking also showed significant usage.

The relatively balanced distribution indicates that customers prefer multiple payment options.

### Average Discount by Product Category

Average discount percentages were compared across product categories.

Sports recorded the highest average discount, while most categories remained within a relatively narrow discount range.

This indicates that the overall discount strategy is relatively consistent across product categories.

### Quarter-wise Final Sales

Quarterly sales were analyzed to understand changes in revenue performance over time.

Sales showed fluctuations across different quarters, with 2023-Q1 recording one of the strongest sales performances.

Quarterly analysis can help identify seasonal patterns and periods of stronger or weaker business performance.

### Return Status by Product Category

Returned and non-returned orders were compared across product categories.

Non-returned orders were significantly higher than returned orders across all categories.

The analysis can help businesses identify categories that may require further investigation regarding product quality, customer expectations, or delivery-related issues.

### Total Quantity Sold by Product Category

Product demand was analyzed based on the total quantity sold.

Furniture recorded the highest quantity sold, followed by other high-demand categories such as Home and Kitchen and Grocery.

This information can support inventory planning and stock management decisions.

## Key Business Insights

The analysis generated several important business insights.

Bengaluru is one of the strongest geographic markets based on final sales.

Grocery is the leading product category based on revenue performance.

Website and Mobile App together contribute the majority of sales-channel revenue, highlighting the importance of digital commerce.

Regular customers are the largest revenue-generating customer segment.

Customer payment preferences are distributed across multiple payment methods, indicating the importance of maintaining diverse payment options.

Discount percentages are relatively consistent across product categories.

Quarterly sales fluctuate over time, indicating potential seasonal or business-cycle effects.

Most customer orders are not returned, which indicates a relatively strong order retention pattern.

Furniture shows strong product demand based on total quantity sold.
